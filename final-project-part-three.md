# Changes made since Part II
Since completing Part II, I made several important improvements to make my story clearer and more audience-focused:

1. **More audience-targeted narrative**
   - I rewrote my storyline so it directly speaks to what everyday diners care about when choosing restaurants.
   - I focused more on practical guidance rather than only describing data patterns.

2. **Clear logical flow between visualizations**
   - I revised my story structure so each chart naturally leads to the next step in the reasoning.
   - This helped build a complete and progressive story: from restaurant category differences → to review behavior → to what readers should actually do.

3. **Refined data visuals**
   - I improved color choices, labels, and layout to make the charts easier to understand at a quick glance.
   - I also added short takeaways on the charts to reduce the effort for interpretation.

4. **Added proper references and sources**
   - I updated citations to make the data more trustworthy and transparent for the audience.


## The audience
My audience is **adults who actively dine out and rely on Yelp before choosing restaurants**.

**Key insights that informed my design:**
- Many people assume that the same star rating carries the same meaning across all restaurant types.
- They prefer visuals and short guidance over long analytical text.
- They want practical recommendations they can use immediately.

**Adjustments made for this audience:**
- Simplified content into quick “rules of thumb”
- Used relatable examples (e.g., sushi vs. fast food)
- Optimized for a clean scrolling phone experience
- Applied warm food-related color palette and consistent iconography


## Final design decisions
Below are some design considerations that significantly shaped the storytelling:

| Category | Decision | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Visual Hierarchy | Strong headline text + annotated charts | Faster comprehension |
| Color & Style | Consistent warm palette | Keeps focus on story, not styling |
| Data Filtering | Focused on top categories by count | Avoids noisy long-tail data |
| Actionability | Created a final "decision chain" | Direct takeaway for users |
| Chart Types | Used bar charts + distribution views | Best for cross-category comparison |

Throughout the process, I learned that **data visualization only works when the audience instantly sees why it matters**.
